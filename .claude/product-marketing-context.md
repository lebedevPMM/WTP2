## Product Overview

**One-liner:** WTP — UAE-based execution partner that builds bankable, tax-compliant business structures and manages real estate investments for international clients through trusted professional intermediaries.

**What it does:** WTP takes clients from intent to outcome in the UAE: company formation, corporate banking, visa/residency, tax residency planning, compliance, substance (real presence), wealth management, and real estate investment. The company operates a "banking-first" model — assessing risk and bank readiness before registration, not after. WTP works primarily through B2B partners (law firms, tax advisors, family offices) who refer clients, and also serves end clients directly.

**Product category:** UAE corporate services / advisory / execution partner (NOT "company formation agent")

**Product type:** Professional services (advisory + execution), B2B partner-driven + B2C direct

**Business model:**
- **Cost + Fee:** Direct costs (government fees, third-party expenses) shown separately and passed through at cost. Management fee charged for architecture, compliance preparation, banking routing, coordination, and responsibility.
- **Packages:** 3 levels — (1) Architecture + banking strategy + compliance + pre-check, (2) + Registration/license + bank onboarding, (3) + Real presence/substance + 3-6 month support
- **Retainer:** Monthly subscription for ongoing compliance, accounting, substance maintenance
- **Real Estate:** Standard broker commission (2% secondary, 2-5% off-plan from developer) + consulting fee for integrated services (visa, tax, structure)

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem (B2B):** Partners have clients who need UAE execution, but can't find a local provider that meets their professional standards. Most UAE "agents" are transactional, opaque, cut corners on compliance, and create reputational risk.

**Core problem (B2C):** Business owners and HNWI face a maze of UAE regulations, banks, free zones, tax implications — and the market is full of providers who promise "fast and easy" but deliver problems months later (bank rejections, tax exposure, substance failures).

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Mass-market company formation agents:** Sell registrations, not outcomes. No banking expertise. Client gets a license but no bank account. No risk assessment.
- **Big Four / large consultancies:** Expensive, slow, bureaucratic, not hands-on. Not practical for mid-market clients.
- **"Grey" operators:** Promise to "solve everything" including sanctions avoidance. Create reputational and legal risk for everyone involved.
- **DIY / freelancers:** Inconsistent quality, no accountability, no institutional knowledge of banking compliance.

**What it costs them:**
- Failed bank applications (months of delay, lost business)
- Tax exposure from incorrect structuring (potentially hundreds of thousands in penalties)
- Reputational damage to the referring partner
- Wasted registration fees and setup costs on structures that don't work
- Stress and time managing multiple fragmented providers

**Emotional tension:**
- Partners: "If this goes wrong, it's my name on the line"
- Clients: "I don't know who to trust — everyone promises the same thing"
- Both: "The cost of getting it wrong is much higher than the cost of getting it right"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:** Other UAE corporate service providers (Sovereign, Amicorp, various local "PRO" companies, boutique consultancies in DIFC/ADGM)
- Fall short because: transactional mindset, sell registrations not outcomes, limited banking expertise, don't filter clients, weak compliance culture

**Secondary competitors:** International advisory firms (Henley & Partners, Astons, large tax firms with UAE desks)
- Fall short because: expensive overhead, slow processes, generalist rather than UAE-specialist, less hands-on execution capability

**Indirect competitors:** "Do nothing" / keep current structure / use a different jurisdiction
- Falls short because: regulatory changes (UK non-dom, German exit tax, NL Box 3) are forcing action; UAE is increasingly the right answer for the right client

**Real Estate specific:** ~8,000 agencies and 27,000+ agents in Dubai market
- Fall short because: pure transaction focus, no integrated consulting (tax, visa, company), volume over quality, no substance expertise

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
1. **Banking-first process:** Pre-check and risk assessment BEFORE registration. No "register first, hope for the best."
2. **Risk-aware filtering:** Explicit ICP and stop-factors. We decline toxic/non-compliant clients. This protects partners.
3. **Partner protection model:** Client ownership rules, non-solicitation, dedicated communication channel, SLA.
4. **Cost + Fee transparency:** No hidden markups on government fees. Clean separation of costs and advisory fee.
5. **Substance as a real product:** Not virtual office theater. Actual management infrastructure, real presence, defensible substance.
6. **Integrated offering:** Banking + company + visa + tax residency + substance + real estate — synchronized as one method, not a menu of disconnected services.

**How we do it differently:** We treat UAE structuring as an engineering problem with specific risk checkpoints, not as a commodity transaction. Every case gets a pre-check, a banking strategy, and a risk verdict before any money is spent on registration.

**Why that's better:** Higher success rate on bank onboarding. Fewer "zombie companies" (registered but non-functional). Partners can recommend with confidence. Clients get working infrastructure, not just documents.

**Why customers choose us:** "Because they told me what they DON'T do, not just what they do. That gave me confidence they'd protect my client."

- Partner got embarrassed by their previous UAE provider's sloppy work

**Habit (keeps them with current approach):**
- "We've always used [provider], switching is effort"
- "Our current provider is cheap and fast" (until it breaks)
- "We don't have enough UAE cases to justify a new relationship"
- Inertia — existing workflows and contacts

**Anxiety (about switching to WTP):**
- "What if they're just another Dubai marketing operation?"

- "What if the process takes too long and my client loses patience?"

## Market Context (2025-2026)

**UK:** Non-dom / remittance basis abolished from April 2025. New 4-year FIG regime for new residents. Creates wave of restructuring demand.

**Germany:** Exit tax tightened since 2022 — deferred payment replaced with installment plan (typically 7 years) + security requirements. Makes relocation an engineering project.

**Netherlands:** Box 3 reform ongoing — changing parameters for fictional yield and exempt thresholds in 2026. Continued pressure on wealth holders.

**UAE:** Federal corporate tax 9% (above AED 375K profit) since June 2023. ESR rolled into corporate tax framework. Banks increasingly strict on compliance. Market of 8,000+ RE agencies / 27,000+ agents.
